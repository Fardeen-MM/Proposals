✔ Voice AI calls every lead in 60 seconds, 24/7 +

You said it yourself: respond within five minutes and you're 21x more likely to close. We call in under 60 seconds.

How it works:
  • Instant trigger: The moment a lead submits a form — from your website, a Google Ad, or a Meta campaign — our system fires. Within 60 seconds, they get a call from your Voice AI assistant.
  • Smart retry logic: No answer? The system waits 25 hours and tries again at a different time. Then again 25 hours later. Three attempts across three days, three different times — maximizing the chance of connection.
  • Parallel SMS: While calling, the AI also sends a text: "Hi, this is Prime Law. We just tried calling about your inquiry. Reply YES if now is a good time." Covers people who don't answer unknown numbers.
  • 24/7/365: A developer needs zoning help at 9pm? They get called at 9pm. A broker sends a referral on Sunday? It's handled before Monday morning. No waiting.
What happens on the call:
  • AI introduces itself as calling from Prime Law
  • Confirms they submitted an inquiry about commercial real estate services
  • Asks qualifying questions specific to their need (entitlements vs. transactional — different criteria for each)
  • Collects key project details: property type, location, timeline, zoning situation
  • Books directly into your calendar
  • Sends confirmation email + calendar invite
Why this matters for you specifically:

You're going to be doing the legal work, managing relationships, and building a firm simultaneously. You can't also be chasing cold leads and playing phone tag. This system means your first interaction with every new prospect is a qualified consultation — not a voicemail callback.

Speed-to-lead studies show that calling within 5 minutes makes you 100x more likely to connect vs. calling after 30 minutes. We call in under 1 minute.

✔ Commercial RE qualification that pre-screens every inquiry +

Not every inquiry is worth 30 minutes of your time. The AI figures out who is before they hit your calendar.

For entitlement & zoning inquiries:
  • Project scope: AI identifies what they want to build or change — restaurant in a non-permitted zone, mixed-use development, variance request
  • Property status: Do they own it? Under contract? Still looking? This tells you how real the deal is.
  • Municipality: Which jurisdiction are we dealing with? This affects complexity and your ability to service them.
  • Timeline urgency: Are they closing next week or "exploring options"? Urgent deals get priority scheduling.
For transactional inquiries (agreements, leases, financing):
  • Deal type: Agreement of sale, lease negotiation, financing documents — AI routes to the right conversation
  • Deal size: Ballpark transaction value helps you prioritize and prepare
  • Timeline: When do they need docs? Next week or next quarter?
  • Existing representation: Are they switching attorneys or starting fresh?
Internal scoring:

Every lead gets scored based on the criteria you set. High-value entitlement project with a tight deadline? That's a priority-one alert to your phone. Someone "just exploring" a potential purchase six months out? They go into a nurture sequence. You decide the rules — we build the logic.

Every practice area gets its own qualification flow. You tell us the criteria, we build the conversation.

Why Mortar Metrics

✔ Full-Funnel Tracking +

Most agencies report "cost per lead" and call it a day. That's like a restaurant measuring how many people walked past the door instead of how many paid for dinner.

What most agencies track:
  • Impressions (how many people saw the ad)
  • Clicks (how many clicked)
  • Cost per lead (how much to get a form fill)

Then they hand you a PDF with pretty graphs and say "we got you 50 leads this month!" But how many of those became clients? They don't know. They don't track that far.

What we track:
  • Lead → Contact rate: Did we actually reach them?
  • Contact → Qualified rate: Were they worth talking to?
  • Qualified → Booked rate: Did they schedule?
  • Booked → Show rate: Did they show up?
  • Show → Close rate: Did they sign?
Why this changes everything:

When we see your booking rate is 28% but your show rate is only 60%, we know exactly where to focus. Maybe the confirmation sequence needs work. Maybe we need to add reminder texts. Maybe we're booking too far out.

When we see one ad set converts leads to retainers at 15% and another at 5%, we kill the loser and scale the winner — not based on CPL, but on actual client acquisition.

Your weekly report includes:
  • Leads generated (by source, by campaign, by ad)
  • Contact rate and best times to reach
  • Qualification rate and common disqualifiers
  • Booking rate and drop-off reasons
  • Show rate and no-show patterns
  • Close rate and revenue generated
  • True CPA and ROI

This is how we knew Agile Legal's true CPA was $672 — not the $4.13 cost per lead that would've been the only number most agencies reported.
