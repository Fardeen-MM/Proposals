✓ Double qualification — no more garbage leads on your phone +

Seven Figure Law sent you 70 leads in 3 months. You closed 2. Walker promised 10 premier leads a month and never delivered more than 4. The problem was single-layer qualification — leads met basic criteria at the ad level, then got dumped on you raw.

Layer 1: Ad-level qualification
  • Targeted campaigns: We collaborate on the exact ICP — demographics, geography, case types — so the ads only reach people likely to need your services
  • Form-level screening: Landing page forms ask initial qualifying questions before someone can submit
  • Geographic targeting: Fort Worth core with expansion across DFW and Texas as you scale
Layer 2: Voice AI deep qualification
  • Accident timeline: Was the accident within the last 12 months?
  • Medical treatment: Have they seen a doctor? Are they currently treating?
  • Insurance verification: Does the at-fault party have insurance? If not, does the lead have uninsured motorist coverage?
  • Existing representation: Do they already have an attorney?
  • Injury severity: What injuries are they dealing with?
Lead scoring logic:
  • Each answer adjusts the lead score in real time
  • Accident within 3 months + treating + insured at-fault party = high priority → you get notified immediately
  • Accident 10 months ago + no treatment + no insurance = low priority → flagged but not booked
  • You define the criteria, we build the logic

Walker and Seven Figure had one layer of qualification. We have two. The leads that reach your calendar have already been screened twice — once by the campaign, once by the AI. No more wading through garbage to find the one case worth taking.

✓ Full-Funnel Tracking +

Seven Figure Law reported "leads generated." Walker reported "premier vs. semi-qualified." Neither could tell you which ad produced the cases you actually signed.

  • Impressions (how many people saw the ad)
  • Clicks (how many clicked)
  • Cost per lead (how much to get a form fill)

Then they hand you a PDF with pretty graphs and say "we got you 50 leads this month!" But how many of those became signed cases? They don't know. They don't track that far.

What we track:
  • Lead → Contact rate: Did we actually reach them?
  • Contact → Qualified rate: Were they worth talking to?
  • Qualified → Booked rate: Did they schedule?

Why this changes everything:

When we see your booking rate is 28% but your show rate is only 60%, we know exactly where to focus. Maybe the confirmation sequence needs work. Maybe we need to add reminder texts. Maybe we're booking too far out.

Your weekly report includes:
  • Leads generated (by source, by campaign, by ad)
  • Contact rate and best times to reach
  • Qualification rate and common disqualifiers
  • Booking rate and drop-off reasons
  • Show rate and no-show patterns
  • Close rate and revenue generated
  • True CPA and ROI

This is how we knew Agile Legal's true CPA was $806 — not the $4.13 cost per lead that would've been the only number Seven Figure or Walker reported.
