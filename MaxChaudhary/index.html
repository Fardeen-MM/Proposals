✓ Voice AI that qualifies — not just routes +

Your current AI answers the phone and forwards messages. Ours asks the right questions, screens out tire-kickers, and books only qualified prospects into your calendar — 24/7/365.

How it works:
  • Instant trigger: The moment a lead submits a form, our system fires. Within 60 seconds, they get a call from your Voice AI assistant.
  • Smart retry logic: No answer? The system waits 25 hours and tries again at a different time. Then again 25 hours later. Three attempts across three days, three different times — maximizing the chance of connection.
  • Parallel SMS: While calling, the AI also sends a text: “Hi, this is Chaudhary Law. We just tried calling about your inquiry. Reply YES if now is a good time.” Covers people who don’t answer unknown numbers.
  • 24/7/365: Airport detentions and deportation matters don’t happen 9-to-5. Neither does our system. Urgent leads at 11pm get called at 11pm.
What happens on the call:
  • AI introduces itself as calling from Chaudhary Law
  • Confirms they submitted an inquiry about legal services
  • Asks qualifying questions specific to their case type (different flows for Federal Court, inadmissibility, appeals — more on that below)
  • Screens out automatic disqualifiers: people seeking ghost consulting, simple visa processing without a legal issue, or those unwilling to pay a consultation fee
  • Explains consultation pricing and collects payment
  • Books directly into the appropriate calendar (Max for complex litigation, associate for initial assessments)
  • Sends confirmation email + calendar invite

Right now, your AI takes a message and puts a name and number on your calendar. When you wake up, you see “missed call from prospect” with no idea if they’re worth calling back.

✓ Practice-specific screening for complex litigation & appeals +

You told us: “Every minute spent in a consultation should be a minute spent on a viable, high-value case.” So every practice area gets its own qualification flow.

For Federal Court Judicial Reviews:
  • Decision identification: AI confirms they have a refusal or negative decision from IRCC
  • Timeline check: How long ago was the decision? Are they within the filing deadline?
  • Prior representation: Are they switching lawyers or starting fresh?
  • Fast-track flagging: Urgent deadlines get priority scheduling with Max
For Inadmissibility (Criminal / Medical):
  • Issue identification: Criminal record? Medical inadmissibility? Misrepresentation allegation?
  • Urgency detection: Are they facing an active enforcement action or removal order?
  • CBSA involvement: Is IRCC or CBSA currently investigating?
  • Complexity gauge: Single issue vs. multiple grounds — helps you prepare
For IRB Appeals (RPD / RAD / IAD):
  • Hearing status: Upcoming hearing? Recent negative decision? Appeal deadline?
  • Division identification: RPD claim, RAD appeal, or IAD sponsorship/residency appeal
  • Existing counsel: Are they unrepresented or seeking new representation?
  • Case urgency: Removal order in effect? Detention? Pre-removal risk assessment needed?
For Business Immigration:
  • Business type: Start-up visa, LMIA, intra-company transfer?
  • Stage assessment: Planning phase or active application?
  • Complexity level: Routine processing or legal issue requiring senior counsel?
  • Routing logic: Routine matters routed to associate; complex issues flagged for Max

Every practice area gets its own qualification flow. You tell us the criteria, we build the conversation.

✓ Full-Funnel Tracking +

Most agencies report “cost per lead” and call it a day. That’s like a restaurant measuring how many people walked past the door instead of how many paid for dinner.

What most agencies track:
  • Impressions (how many people saw the ad)
  • Clicks (how many clicked)
  • Cost per lead (how much to get a form fill)

Then they hand you a PDF with pretty graphs and say “we got you 50 leads this month!” But how many of those became clients? They don’t know. They don’t track that far.

What we track:
  • Lead → Contact rate: Did we actually reach them?
  • Contact → Qualified rate: Were they worth talking to?
  • Qualified → Booked rate: Did they schedule?
  • Booked → Show rate: Did they show up?
  • Show → Close rate: Did they sign?
Why this changes everything:

When we see your booking rate is 28% but your show rate is only 60%, we know exactly where to focus. Maybe the confirmation sequence needs work. Maybe we need to add reminder texts. Maybe we’re booking too far out.

When we see one ad set converts leads to retainers at 15% and another at 5%, we kill the loser and scale the winner — not based on CPL, but on actual client acquisition.

Your weekly report includes:
  • Leads generated (by source, by campaign, by ad)
  • Contact rate and best times to reach
  • Qualification rate and common disqualifiers
  • Booking rate and drop-off reasons
  • Show rate and no-show patterns
  • Close rate and revenue generated
  • True CPA and ROI — broken down by practice area

This is how we knew Agile Legal’s true CPA was $672 — not the $4.13 cost per lead that would’ve been the only number most agencies reported.
